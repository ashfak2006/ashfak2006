Balancing Faith and Career in Tech: My Personal Journey

"And say, ‘Do [as you will], for Allah will see your deeds.’" — Surah At-Tawbah (9:105)

Introduction: One Life, Many Roles

My name is Ashfak Ameer and like many young people chasing a dream, I wear many hats. I’m a second-year Computer Application student at Calicut University, a self-taught tech enthusiast, a weekend catering worker, a gym-goer, and—most importantly—a Muslim trying to stay connected to my faith. Balancing all these parts of my life isn’t easy. There are days when deadlines, classes, and work schedules feel overwhelming. But through it all, I’ve learned one powerful truth: when you prioritize your faith, everything else starts to fall into place

The Tech Dream and the Faith Foundation

As someone coming from a middle-class family I’ve always had to work hard for what I want. I didn’t grow up with access to expensive gadgets or coaching classes. But I had ambition, and I had YouTube, free courses, and the will to learn.
While others spent their weekends relaxing, I was either working in catering to earn something, or sitting with my laptop teaching myself python Django, HTML, CSS, or JavaScript. Sometimes I’d study after coming back from physical work, tired but still motivated.
Like everyone else, my ambition is to be rich. I work hard for that goal every day. My dream is to land a high-paying tech job after completing my degree—not just to earn money, but to support my family, grow as a professional, and serve a greater purpose.
In all this, my faith was never on pause.

The Discipline of Faith Meets the Discipline of Tech

Learning tech is not just about skills. It requires patience, consistency, and problem-solving. Funny enough, so does practicing Islam

"Indeed, with hardship comes ease." — Surah Ash-Sharh (94:6)

That verse has carried me through bugs, stress, and burnout.

Faith Isn’t a Barrier — It’s My Superpower

  • Keeps me grounded
  • Pushes me to be ethical in work
  • Reminds me to serve others
  • Gives meaning to my efforts

Final Thoughts

I’m still growing. I’m still learning. I’m not perfect But every line of code I write, every step I take in the gym, every plate I carry in catering, every lecture I attend, is part of a mission.

"Indeed, Allah loves when any of you does a job, he does it with excellence." — Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

I want to do my job well, whether it’s coding, serving food, or praying. I want to be a good son, a good student, and a good man.
So here’s to all the young dreamers out there: keep your faith strong, work hard, and never lose sight of what matters most.

May Allah guide us all in our journeys.

Ashfak Ameer
