My Self-Learning Journey in Tech

Introduction

In a world where access to knowledge is just a click away, I decided to take ownership of my future. My name is Ashfak Ameer, and I’m a second-year Computer Application student at Calicut University. I come from a humble, middle-class background and work part-time in catering on weekends. Despite a busy schedule that includes studies, physical work, and daily gym routines, I made a decision: to learn tech on my own and build a meaningful career

How It All Began

My self-learning journey began with the Brototype 100K Learning Challenge on YouTube. It opened my eyes to the endless possibilities of web development and the power of consistent effort. From there, I explored platforms like W3Schools for foundational web skills and followed various YouTube channels to deepen my understanding of programming and frameworks.

What made my journey smoother was the smart use of AI tools like ChatGPT and others that helped me debug code, explore new technologies, and save time on problem-solving. I treated every problem as an opportunity to learn rather than a reason to give up.

Balancing Life and Learning

Self-learning didn’t mean I had extra time. In fact, I had to fight for it. I worked catering shifts on weekends, went to college during the day, and committed to regular gym sessions to stay mentally and physically sharp. But even with all this, I carved out time daily to learn, practice, and build projects.

This discipline has become one of my strongest assets. It has taught me that consistency beats talent when talent doesn’t show up.

What I Built Along the Way

Over time, I applied what I learned into real-world projects. Some of the key ones include:

  • E-commerce Website: Built using Django, including product listings,cart, and checkout features.
  • Custom AI Chatbot:A context-aware chatbot using FAISS and sentence transformers.
  • Portfolio Website: A site that showcases my work, values, and vision for the future.

These projects weren’t just academic exercises—they were proof that I was growing, building, and solving problems.

Advice for Others Starting Out

To anyone from a similar background or struggling with time and resources, here’s my message:


  • Start with free content. YouTube and platforms like W3Schools can take you a long way.
  • Use AI tools to your advantage. They can help you learn faster and solve problems.
  • Consistency is key. Even 30 minutes a day adds up over time.
  • Build projects, no matter how small. They are your best teachers.
  • Stay disciplined. Balance is possible with the right mindset and planning.
  • Trust your journey. Everyone starts somewhere.

Final Thoughts

This journey has not only given me technical skills but also built my character. I’ve learned how to manage time, how to push through exhaustion, and how to stay focused on long-term goals. I’ve learned to trust in Allah’s plan while putting in my best effort every day.

"Indeed, Allah loves when any of you does a job, he does it with excellence." — Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

So here’s to all the young dreamers out there: keep your faith strong, work hard, and never lose sight of what matters most.


I hope my journey encourages others to start theirs. You don’t need to wait for the perfect course or expensive bootcamp. All you need is a reason, a routine, and the resilience to keep going..
